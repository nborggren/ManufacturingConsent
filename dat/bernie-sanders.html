Top Priorities

  • Taking care of the planet: Heed the warnings of scientists with regards to climate change.
  • Fair taxation: Corporations should not be taxed as people.
  • Employ young people: Fund training programs and job creation for America's young adults through the Employ Young Americans Now Act.
  • The minimum wage: Incrementally increase it with the goal of reaching $15 per hour.
  • Campaign finance reform: Control campaign spending.
  • Protecting Americans' privacy: Do away with the “Orwellian” Patriot Act.

Bernie’s Resumé

  • 1960-1964
    Attended the University of Chicago.
  • 1964-1971
    Worked as a carpenter, documentary filmmaker, and researcher in Vermont.
  • 1971
    Joined the anti-Vietnam War Liberty Union Party.
  • 1972
    Unsuccessful senatorial and gubernatorial campaign.
  • 1974
    Unsuccessful senatorial campaign.
  • 1976
    Unsuccessful gubernatorial campaign.
  • 1979-1981
    Worked as a writer and as the director of the American People's Historical Society.
  • 1981-1989
    Mayor of Burlington, Vermont.
  • 1990-2007
    U.S. Congressman from Vermont
  • 2006-Present
    U.S. Senator from Vermont.
  • 2013-2015
    Chairman of the Senate Veterans' Affairs Committee.

Highs

  • 2010: Gave an 8-hour speech opposing the extension of Bush-era tax breaks that was turned into a book.
  • 2007: Co-wrote the Green Jobs Act with Hillary Clinton.
  • 1991: Co-founded the Congressional Progressive Caucus.
  • 1984: Established the Burlington Community Land Trust, which provided discounted housing to the middle class.
