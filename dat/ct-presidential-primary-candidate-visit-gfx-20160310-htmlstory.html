Presidential candidates are converging on the Chicago area to drum up support for their campaigns in the days before Tuesday's primary election in Illinois.

Friday will see Chicago-area events from Bernie Sanders, Donald Trump and Ted Cruz. Democratic presidential front-runner Hillary Clinton was in the suburbs Thursday for one last push.

Where candidates are making appearances

Bernie Sanders

Where: Argo Community High School, 7329 63rd St., Summit.

What: "A Future to Believe In" rally. The campaign said Sanders will talk about issues ranging from making public colleges and universities tuition-free to combating climate change. Doors open at 5 p.m. Friday.

Are tickets required? No, but RSVPs are encouraged for the free event. Admission is first-come, first-served. No word from the campaign yet on capacity for the event, but organizers are encouraging people to avoid bringing bags for security reasons. Signs or banners on sticks will not be allowed through security.

Donald Trump

Where: University of Illinois at Chicago Pavilion, 525 S. Racine Ave., Chicago.

What: Doors open at 3 p.m. Friday; event starts at 6 p.m. A protest rally is planned as well; it has thousands of Facebook RSVPs. The parking lot south of the pavilion on Harrison Street will be open for demonstrations.

Are tickets required? Yes, but they are free. The pavilion seats 9,500 people.

Ted Cruz

Where: Palmer House Hilton, 17 E. Monroe St., Chicago.

What: 2nd annual Governor’s Dinner. Cruz is a confirmed guest for the event, which starts at 5:30 p.m. Friday with a private reception and photo opportunity followed by dinner.

Are tickets required? Yes, tickets are available and start at $500 per person.

Where: The Meadows Club, 2950 W. Golf Road, Rolling Meadows.

What: The 9th annual Northwest Suburban Republican Lincoln Day Dinner hosted by townships of Schaumburg, Barrington, Elk Grove, Hanover, Maine, Northfield, Palatine and Wheeling. Cruz is the keynote speaker for the event, which starts at 6:30 p.m. Friday with a reception followed by dinner.

Are tickets required? Yes, but they are sold out.
