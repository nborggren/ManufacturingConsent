Hillary Clinton will pay a return visit to "Jimmy Kimmel Live" on March 24. 

It will be the democratic front-runner's second appearance on the ABC late-night show. She last stopped by in November, and has also graced the couches of "The Late Show With Stephen Colbert," "The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon" and "Late Night With Seth Meyers" during this election cycle.

Perhaps taking a cue from media-savvy President Obama, who in recent months has spoken at South by Southwest and appeared on "Comedians in Cars Getting Coffee" with Jerry Seinfeld, Clinton seems more willing to venture into nontraditional venues. Case in point: a cameo Wednesday on the Comedy Central series "Broad City."
