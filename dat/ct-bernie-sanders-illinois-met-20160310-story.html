Bernie Sanders announces Friday rally as candidates converge on Chicago

William LeeContact ReporterChicago Tribune

Democratic presidential candidate Bernie Sanders will speak in the Chicago area Friday, the same day that leading Republican candidates Donald Trump and Ted Cruz also will be campaigning here in the run-up to next week's Illinois primary.

Fresh off Sanders' stunning primary win over Hillary Clinton in Michigan this week, his campaign announced Thursday that he will head to Argo Community High School in west suburban Summit for a Friday rally. Doors open at 5 p.m., the campaign said.

Sanders was in the Chicago area late last month for a large event at Chicago State University, a predominantly African-American public school in danger of closing because of the budget impasse in Springfield. A Tribune poll taken March 2-6 found Sanders trailing Clinton by 42 percentage points in Illinois.

Clinton is in town Thursday night for a rally in north suburban Vernon Hills. Earlier this week, former President Bill Clinton campaigned on his wife's behalf on the city's West Side and in Evanston.

On Friday evening, Trump will hold a rally at the University of Illinois at Chicago Pavilion. Cruz will be in town Friday for a $500-per-person fundraiser for Illinois Republicans at the Palmer House Hilton and for the Northwest Suburban Republican Lincoln Day Dinner in Rolling Meadows. The Tribune poll found Cruz trailing Trump by 10 points in the state.
